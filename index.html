Abstract

This paper presents a new version of OpenRatSLAM (called OpenRatSLAM2) designed for unmanned surface vehicle (USV) applications. It leverages the ROS2 framework to provide low-computational cost, bioinspired SLAM suitable for GPS-denied environments. We contribute a ROS2-based architecture, experimental results on two waterway datasets, and insights into visual‚Äìinertial SLAM tuning. This work represents the first known application of RatSLAM to USVs.
